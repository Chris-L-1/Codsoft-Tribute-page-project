BIOGRAPHY

Christiaan Neethling Barnard (1922â€“2001) was a South African cardiac surgeon who performed the first human-to-human heart transplant on December 3, 1967. Despite the patient, Louis Washkansky, dying 18 days later due to pneumonia caused by anti-rejection drugs, Barnard's second patient lived for 18 months. Born in Beaufort West, Barnard developed a life-saving technique for intestinal atresia as a young doctor. After studying in the U.S., he returned to South Africa and led the Department of Experimental Surgery at Groote Schuur Hospital. He retired in 1983 due to rheumatoid arthritis and later promoted an anti-aging cream that was discredited. He founded the Christiaan Barnard Foundation to help underprivileged children and died in 2001 from an asthma attack.
